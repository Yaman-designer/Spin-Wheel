How can I customize promotion texts?

Validated on 15 Jan 2024 • Last edited on 27 Jun 2025

Overview

The Texts section allows you to customize all the text content that appears in your promotion popup. This includes the headline, description, disclaimer, button labels, and close link text. Customizing these texts helps you create a promotion that matches your brand voice and communicates effectively with your audience. This guide will show you how to edit and optimize all text elements.

Accessing Text Settings

  1. Log in to your Wheelluck dashboard.
  2. Navigate to the Promotions section from the left-hand menu.
  3. Click on the promotion you want to edit, or create a new promotion.
  4. On the promotion editing page, locate the Texts accordion section.
  5. Click on the Texts accordion to expand it and reveal all text input fields.

Available Text Fields

1. Headline

Purpose: The main title or heading that appears at the top of your promotion popup.

  • This is the first thing users see when the promotion appears.
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • This field is required and cannot be left empty.
  • Appears prominently at the top of the promotion popup.
Tip: Keep your headline short, catchy, and attention-grabbing. Use action words and create a sense of urgency or excitement. Examples: "Special Bonus Unlocked!", "Win Big Today!", "Spin to Save!"

2. Description

Purpose: A brief explanation or call-to-action that appears below the headline.

  • Provides more context about the promotion and encourages users to participate.
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • This field is optional but recommended for better engagement.
  • Appears between the headline and the disclaimer.
Tip: Use the description to explain the value proposition and create excitement. Keep it concise but informative. Examples: "You have a chance to win amazing discounts. Are you feeling lucky? Give it a spin!", "Spin the wheel and unlock exclusive deals just for you!"

3. Disclaimer

Purpose: Important terms, conditions, or rules that users should know before participating.

  • Typically includes information about participation limits, coupon validity, and terms of use.
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • This field is optional but recommended for legal clarity and user expectations.
  • Appears below the description, usually in smaller text.
Tip: Include important information like participation limits, coupon expiration times, and any restrictions. Use line breaks (Enter key) to separate different points for better readability. Examples: "You can spin the wheel only once.\nIf you win, coupon can be claimed for 15 minutes only!\nSame email must be used when ordering."

4. Submit Button

Purpose: The text that appears on the main action button that users click to spin the wheel.

  • This is the primary call-to-action button in your promotion.
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • This field is required and cannot be left empty.
  • Appears as the main button in the promotion popup.
Tip: Use action-oriented, exciting text that encourages clicks. Keep it short and clear. Examples: "Try your luck", "Spin Now", "Get Started", "Win Now", "Let's Go!"

5. Close Link

Purpose: The text that appears on the link/button that allows users to close or dismiss the promotion popup.

  • Provides users with an option to decline the promotion.
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • This field is required and cannot be left empty.
  • Appears as a clickable link, usually at the bottom of the popup.
Tip: Use polite, non-pushy text that respects user choice. Examples: "No, I don't feel lucky", "Maybe later", "Not interested", "Skip this offer", "Close"

How to Edit Texts

  1. Open the Texts section in your promotion editor.
  2. Locate the text field you want to edit (Headline, Description, Disclaimer, Submit Button, or Close Link).
  3. Click in the input field or textarea to place your cursor.
  4. Type or paste your desired text.
  5. For textarea fields (Description and Disclaimer), you can press Enter to create line breaks.
  6. As you type, you'll see a live preview of your changes in the promotion preview panel on the right side of the screen.
  7. Pay attention to character limits - the system will prevent you from entering more than the maximum allowed characters.
  8. Click Save to save your text changes permanently.
Live Preview: Changes to text fields are immediately reflected in the preview panel, so you can see how your promotion will look in real-time as you edit.

Character Limits and Requirements

Field Type Maximum Length Required
Headline Text Input 100 characters Yes
Description Textarea 100 characters No
Disclaimer Textarea 100 characters No
Submit Button Text Input 100 characters Yes
Close Link Text Input 100 characters Yes
Required Fields: Headline, Submit Button, and Close Link are required fields and must be filled in before you can save your promotion. Description and Disclaimer are optional but recommended.

Best Practices for Writing Effective Texts

Headline Best Practices

  • Be Clear and Direct: Users should immediately understand what the promotion is about.
  • Create Urgency: Use words like "Now", "Today", "Limited Time" to create a sense of urgency.
  • Highlight Value: Mention the benefit or reward users can get.
  • Keep It Short: With a maximum of 100 characters, be concise and impactful.
  • Use Action Words: Words like "Win", "Unlock", "Get", "Claim" are more engaging.

Description Best Practices

  • Explain the Value: Tell users what they can win or gain.
  • Create Excitement: Use enthusiastic language that builds anticipation.
  • Include a Call to Action: Encourage users to participate.
  • Be Conversational: Write in a friendly, approachable tone.
  • Keep It Relevant: Make sure the description matches your actual rewards.

Disclaimer Best Practices

  • Be Transparent: Clearly state any limitations or restrictions.
  • Include Important Details: Mention participation limits, coupon validity periods, and any requirements.
  • Use Line Breaks: Separate different points with line breaks for better readability.
  • Be Concise: Include only essential information to stay within the 100-character limit.
  • Set Expectations: Help users understand what to expect if they win.

Button Text Best Practices

  • Submit Button: Use exciting, action-oriented text that encourages clicks. Make it feel like an opportunity, not an obligation.
  • Close Link: Use polite, respectful text that doesn't make users feel guilty for declining. This builds trust and respect for your brand.
  • Keep It Short: Both buttons have character limits, so be concise.
  • Match Your Brand Voice: Ensure button text aligns with your overall brand personality.

Text Placement in Promotion

Understanding where each text appears in your promotion helps you write more effectively:

  1. Headline: Appears at the very top of the promotion popup, in the largest, most prominent text.
  2. Description: Appears directly below the headline, providing context and encouragement.
  3. Disclaimer: Appears below the description, usually in smaller text, before the email input field.
  4. Submit Button: Appears as the main, prominent button below the email input field.
  5. Close Link: Appears as a smaller link, usually at the bottom of the popup, allowing users to dismiss the promotion.
Visual Hierarchy: The order and size of text elements create a visual hierarchy that guides users through your promotion. Make sure your most important message is in the headline, and supporting details are in the description.

Important Notes

  • Character Limits: Pay attention to character limits for each field. The system will prevent you from entering more characters than allowed.
  • Required Fields: Headline, Submit Button, and Close Link must be filled in before you can save your promotion.
  • Live Preview: All text changes are immediately visible in the preview panel, so you can see how they look before saving.
  • Line Breaks: In Description and Disclaimer textarea fields, you can press Enter to create line breaks for better formatting.
  • Save Your Changes: Always remember to click the Save button after editing texts to ensure the changes are saved permanently.
  • Brand Consistency: Ensure your texts match your brand voice and tone across all fields.
  • Mobile Display: Keep in mind that text may appear differently on mobile devices, so test your promotion on different screen sizes.

Troubleshooting

Cannot Save - Required Field Error

  • Make sure Headline, Submit Button, and Close Link fields are filled in.
  • These fields cannot be left empty.
  • Enter text in all required fields and try saving again.

Cannot Type More Characters

  • You've reached the maximum character limit for that field.
  • Check the character limit for the field you're editing. All text fields have a maximum of 100 characters.
  • Edit your text to fit within the character limit.
  • Consider using shorter words or more concise phrasing.

Text Not Appearing in Preview

  • Make sure you've actually entered text in the field (not just spaces).
  • Check that the field is not hidden or disabled.
  • Try clicking in the field again and typing.
  • Refresh the page if the preview doesn't update.

Line Breaks Not Working in Description/Disclaimer

  • Make sure you're using the textarea fields (Description and Disclaimer), not the text input fields.
  • Press Enter to create line breaks in textarea fields.
  • Line breaks will appear in the preview and on the actual promotion.

Text Appears Cut Off or Truncated

  • Check if your text exceeds the character limit for that field.
  • The system may truncate text that's too long.
  • Edit your text to fit within the character limit.
  • On mobile devices, very long text may wrap differently - test on different screen sizes.

Special Characters or Emojis Not Displaying Correctly

  • Some special characters or emojis may not render correctly in all browsers or devices.
  • Test your promotion on different browsers and devices to ensure compatibility.
  • Consider using standard text characters for better compatibility.

Changes Not Saving

  • Make sure you've filled in all required fields (Headline, Submit Button, Close Link).
  • Check for any validation errors that might prevent saving.
  • Click the Save button after making your changes.
  • Wait for the save confirmation message before navigating away.
  • Try refreshing the page and making the changes again if the issue persists.
